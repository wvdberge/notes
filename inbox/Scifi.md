Here are five books that deliver the cyberpunk atmosphere and the philosophical, hard-sci-fi depth of 2001 and The Three-Body Problem, with a more complex, literary style:
​Neuromancer by William Gibson: The defining cyberpunk novel. Its prose is dense, poetic, and highly atmospheric, plunging you into a gritty, high-tech underworld.
​Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep? by Philip K. Dick: The direct source material for Blade Runner. It leans heavily into the psychological and philosophical questions of empathy, reality, and artificial life.
​Blindsight by Peter Watts: A dark, intellectually rigorous hard sci-fi novel about first contact. It aggressively explores the nature of consciousness and will appeal directly to the cerebral side of 2001 and The Three-Body Problem.
​Hyperion by Dan Simmons: Structured like The Canterbury Tales in space. It offers the grand, galaxy-spanning scale of Foundation or The Expanse, but with significantly richer prose and complex, intertwined narratives.
​The Windup Girl by Paolo Bacigalupi: A gritty, award-winning biopunk novel (cyberpunk's biological cousin) set in a dystopian future Thailand, exploring corporate greed and artificial life with sharp literary flair.
