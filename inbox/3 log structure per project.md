That's a really clean split actually — almost maps directly onto the two audiences. The messy log is for you and future-you mid-project, the clean log is proto-paper material that just needs narrative added later.

You could even add a third — a decisions log. Just the key forks in the road: "chose X over Y because Z." That's often the hardest thing to reconstruct when writing up, and it's distinct from both dead ends and results. Reviewers and collaborators always want to know why you did it the way you did, and that reasoning evaporates fastest from memory.

Three logs, three purposes — exploration, decisions, results. Claude Code could probably maintain all three in parallel without much overhead if you set it up well, routing new entries to the right one.

Might be worth spending an hour designing that structure deliberately rather than letting it emerge — given you're only two months in there's still time to retrofit it without too much pain.
