This 13-day itinerary follows a West to East trajectory along the Hadrian's Cycleway (NCN 72), starting with your arrival from IJmuiden and finishing back at the ferry terminal. It averages roughly 30–40 km per cycling day to accommodate a Thule trailer.
Phase 1: Arrival & The Solway Coast
Day 1: Departure from IJmuiden
Board the late afternoon DFDS ferry. Ensure your Thule is in stroller mode for boarding and storage.
Day 2: Newcastle to Carlisle
 * Route: Arrival at the DFDS Newcastle Terminal. Cycle or take a taxi ~15 km to Newcastle Central Station.
 * Logistics: Take the train to Carlisle.
 * Rest: Afternoon rest in Carlisle.
Day 3: Solway Coast Loop (~52 km)
 * Route: Out-and-back to Bowness-on-Solway following NCN 72.
 * Terrain: Exceptionally flat, quiet paved roads through salt marshes.
 * Highlight: Touching the gateway at the official western end of Hadrian's Wall.
Phase 2: Heading East into the Countryside
Day 4: Carlisle to Brampton (~25 km)
 * Route: Leaving Carlisle on NCN 72, heading northeast.
 * Terrain: Gentle, rolling hills on paved minor roads.
 * Activity: Half-day ride, allowing for a relaxed afternoon in Brampton.
Day 5: Brampton Discovery Loop (~30 km)
 * Route: A local loop visiting Lanercost Priory and Talkin Tarn Country Park.
 * Terrain: Mixed paved lanes and firm park paths around the lake.
 * Activity: Exploring the 12th-century priory ruins and a lakeside play break for your child.
Day 6: Brampton to Haltwhistle (~28 km)
 * Route: NCN 72 towards the "Centre of Britain."
 * Terrain: Increasing gradients as you approach the National Park.
 * Highlight: Passing the Roman Army Museum at Walltown.
Phase 3: The Roman Heart & Gravel Detours
Day 7: South Tyne Gravel Loop (~42 km)
 * Route: Haltwhistle to Alston and back via the South Tyne Trail.
 * Terrain: Hard-packed gravel and cinder on a former railway line.
 * Highlight: Riding over the Lambley Viaduct South Tyne Trail Car Park for panoramic valley views.
Day 8: Haltwhistle to Hexham (~35 km)
 * Route: The most challenging section of NCN 72, following the "Military Road."
 * Terrain: Significant short, sharp hills.
 * Stops: You will pass the major archaeological sites of Vindolanda and Housesteads Roman Fort - Vercovicium - English Heritage Site.
Day 9: Hexham Rest Day
 * Activity: No cycling. Visit Hexham Abbey or enjoy the local parks and playgrounds.
Phase 4: The River Tyne to the Sea
Day 10: Hexham to Newcastle (~40 km)
 * Route: Following the River Tyne east on NCN 72.
 * Terrain: Mostly downhill or flat, transitioning to firm gravel waggonways.
 * Highlight: Riding under the iconic bridges as you enter the Newcastle Quayside.
Day 11: Newcastle Rest Day
 * Activity: Explore the city center, Discovery Museum, or Great North Museum using the Thule in stroller mode.
Day 12: Newcastle to Ferry (~15 km)
 * Route: A final short, flat ride on traffic-free paths along the north bank of the Tyne to North Shields.
 * Logistics: Board the afternoon ferry back to the Netherlands.
Day 13: Arrival in IJmuiden
Ferry arrives in the morning for your final journey home.
